Choice

Footstep by footstep, along its bank
Never one out of line
Follow the footpath and soon you’ll be home
The everlasting routine of life

Taunting river, with its roars and calls
The girl pays it no heed
Beautiful river, with its snow white foam
So enticing, beguiling, alluring—

Silly girl.
Water isn’t alluring.
Water is nothing new.

Silly girl.
These thoughts are foolish.
You have everything you need.

To the grocer’s shop is a long walk
On the path down the flower beds
Ten pence in pocket, five in hand
And the blackbird’s song, always the same

Tempting flowers, swaying in the wind
The girl tries to pay them no heed
Enchanting flowers, full of color
So wonderful, exciting, new—

Foolish girl.
You’ve seen flowers.
Mother grows many at home.

Foolish girl.
This is all unnecessary.
You have everything you need.

Girl at home, by the window
Staring at the great outdoors
She has seen them lots, been there more
Yet she still longs

Pitiable girl.
You’re crazy.
What is there to long for?

Pitiable girl.
So privileged, so blessed.
You have everything you need.

Tired girl.
You come home to food.
A warm fireplace.

Tired girl.
You have been well-rested.
The tiredness doesn’t leave.

Working girl.
Simple, quick.
You’ve done it a million times.

Working girl.
Same old, same old.
Does nobody ever ask?

Longing girl.
You have it all.
But why do you feel this way?

Longing girl.
Everything in your hands.
Yet you want the same.

Girl that needs.
What? She doesn’t know.

Girl that wants.
What? She couldn’t care less.

Girl that’s missing.
What? She wishes to know.

For though she has everything,
She has nothing
Both, all at once.



Description

I wrote "Choice" a fair amount of time after I wrote "Leatherbound Pages". To this day, it is one of my favorite poems.

The perspective switches from third to second, from speaking about the girl to the narrator (whose tone I love) reprimanding the girl. This poem's meaning is in its title; it's simply "choice". The last three lines are probably my favorites, because their simplicity is exactly what I feel this poem needs. I hope you enjoyed it!
