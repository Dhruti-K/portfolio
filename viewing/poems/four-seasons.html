It was spring
And the flowers bloomed bright
And children ran amongst
The verdant grass

It was spring
And the people gathered
With all those they loved
And they laughed

It was spring
The air was light
And the people were joyous
All was very well

It was summer
The heat was strong
And the plants were not
And the people were even less so

It was winter

Out of hiding, once more

It was winter

But nothing is still better
Than the fear

And then the leaves
Shot up from the trees
And the people began
To smile

There was joy again
And dancing and merriment
And the sweet sugared chimes

The fear was not truly gone
But it was never again seen
The flames were only light
And the heat was only warmth

It was not as it once was
