Porcelain Doll

I feel their eyes bore into me
Have they no shame?
They stare as if I am a show
As if I am a lion, jumping through hoops
As if I am a diamond, in a case of glass
As if I am not a person myself

I am respected, they have said
I am above them, I am better
Yet in their eyes, there is no respect
If anything, there is ridicule
And maybe if I were one of them
I would ridicule, as well

Men stand like pillars of iron
Like the bars of a bird’s cage
For of course, even when I am outside
I am still behind a wall
And even as I walk
I am being carried along

Every movement is watched
Every movement is careful
For if I take a wrong step
I may fall, they think, I may shatter
They treat me like a porcelain doll
As if I am made of glass

The river is full of beauty
Not the beauty that I hold
But raw beauty
The beauty of nature
Beauty with unrelenting power
What I could never be

I take a step on one of the stones
I take a step on the next
I take a step and it is not on a stone
I take a step and I fall
But as I land, I do not shatter
I simply bleed

Everyone worries.
Everyone says that this is terrible.
They say that this was foolish.
A terrible idea.
But I do not.
I don’t see why.

They fuss over me
As if I am not capable
They fuss over me
As if I am too meek
Too weak, too inexperienced
To handle a wound so small

And I think that the people
Are right
To ridicule.

They treat me as if I am a porcelain doll
But people aren’t made of glass
