World in Flames

Dewdrop fell from tender leaf
To the parched ground below
Gently, with a certain grace
As the leaf swayed in the wind

Dusk settled on the atmosphere
The moon rising high
Stars hidden behind thickets
Of dense, gray smoke

There was odd beauty in the fire
As it swallowed the spindly grass
As it turned the vibrant, verdant hills
To an expanse of muddied ash

There was an elegance in the flickering flames
As they engulfed the ruins of wood
The remnants of the world before
All gone, without a witness

The trees, once of great majesty
Fell victim to the destruction
Reduced to naught but a memory
In the minds of people past

The flowers, once of great splendor
Were prey to the preying blaze
And though the colors, so bright, were forever gone
The embers brought a sense of peace

Dewdrop fell from tender leaf
Life, in pure desolation
A sign that soon, in due time
The world will be built again.
