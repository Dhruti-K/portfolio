Phoenix

Bird of feathers as bright as the sun
As radiant as a thousand stars
With an entrancing song
And a crown of red
And all the smugness of a king

Bird of dulled orange shade
Fit comfortably in my arms
Its calls were milder
Its regality was abandoned
But it was beautiful, all the same

Bird of unremarkable manner
Gone was its extravagant plumage
It cawed for my attention
But I passed it away
And it slept more than it was awake

Bird that was barely a bird any longer
And of rather unpleasant color
It sat all day by the edge of its cage
From the corner of my eye, I watched it limp
And watched it fall, so often

Gone was the bird, so quiet, so fast
One moment, it was there, then not
No feathers, no caws, no miserable lump
And so I sifted my hand through the soot
And waited for it to rise

But it never did rise, the bird
And I never did know why
